Japanese Boxwood Bonsai Tree

$ 175
Due to its dense foliage, small leaves, and ease of shaping, Japanese Boxwood (Buxus microphylla var. japonica), a slow-growing evergreen shrub native to Japan, is a favorite for bonsai culture. Japanese boxwood bonsai can be fashioned in a variety of ways, such as formal and casual group plantings, slanting styles, and upright styles. They are tolerant of a wide variety of temperatures and relative humidity levels and appreciate bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. Japanese Boxwood bonsai can be vulnerable to pests like boxwood mites and boxwood blight and need frequent pruning and shaping to retain their intended form and compact size. They are a traditional option for bonsai aficionados of all skill levels and can live for many years with the right care.
